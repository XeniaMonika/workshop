Why Digital Methods?

  1. Access to Large Datasets
  2. Exploration of Large-Scale Patterns
Close Reading: A detailed analysis of individual texts, focusing on careful examination of language, structure, and meaning in specific passages or entire works.
Distant Reading: A computational approach to analyzing large text collections, using digital tools to identify patterns, trends, and relationships across multiple texts without close individual reading.

Why Digital Methods?

  1. Improved accessibility of research data
  2. Increased efficiency
  3. Interdisciplinary collaboration
  4. New forms of scholarship
  5. Preservation of cultural heritage

How to start?

You need texts in a machine-readable format!

  1. Use existing corpora and repositories.
  • OpenITI (Arabic and Persian texts from the 7th to the 20th century)
  • Maktaba Shamela (Arabic texts from the 7th to the 20th century)
  • Hindawi (Modern Arabic literature)
  • Nuṣūṣ (Classical Arabic texts, mostly on Sufism and falsafa)
  • Aldiwan (Arabic poetry)
  • Ganjoor (classical Persian poetry)

Text-Preprocessing

  • Plain text - .txt
  • Tokenized text - most tools do it for you
  • Lemmatized text - knows, knew, known as know
  • Annotated text
    • Automatic annotation (e.g. POS-tagging, NER)
    • Manual annotation (e.g. interactions between entities in a text)
  • Stop word list, normalization of orthography, removal of numbers, punctuation, etc.
