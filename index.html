Close Reading

Distant Reading

A detailed analysis of individual texts, focusing on careful examination of language, structure, and meaning in specific passages or entire works.
A computational approach to analyzing large text collections, using digital tools to identify patterns, trends, and relationships across multiple texts without close individual reading.

Why Digital Methods?

  1. Improved accessibility of research data
  2. Increased efficiency
  3. Interdisciplinary collaboration
  4. New forms of scholarship
  5. Preservation of cultural heritage

How to start?

You need texts in a machine-readable format (TXT, CSV, XML, JSON)!

  1. Use texts published on websites - copy-paste or scrape them (Beautiful Soup, Selenium)
  2. Digitize the texts yourself - OCR, HTR. (eScriptorium, Transkribus)
  3. Use existing corpora and repositories:
  • OpenITI (corpus, metadata search): Arabic and Persian texts from the 7th to the 20th century
  • Maktaba Shamela: Arabic texts from the 7th to the 20th century
  • Hindawi: modern Arabic literature
  • Nuṣūṣ: classical Arabic texts, mostly on Sufism and falsafa
  • Aldiwan: Arabic poetry
  • Ganjoor: classical Persian poetry

Text Visualization and Concordances

Definition: Techniques for visualizing text patterns (e.g., word frequencies, keyword contexts) to gain insight into structure and content without detailed reading.

Research Question (RQ) 1: How does the word hikma (wisdom) appear in various contexts across different Sufi treatises?

RQ 2: What recurring concepts and themes emerge in the foundational texts of the Mu'tazilite tradition, and how are they interconnected?

Tools: Voyant Tools, AntConc

Examples: Mummelthei, V. "The Sea and the Beloved"

Topic Modeling

Definition: A computational technique used to identify and group recurring themes or "topics" in a collection of texts based on word co-occurrence patterns.

RQ 1: What are the dominant themes in medieval Islamic legal texts, and how do they evolve across different centuries?

RQ 2: Which topics dominate Sufi poetry, and how do they differ across regions such as Persia, the Maghreb, and South Asia?

Tools: Voyant Tools, Mallet, TopicsExplorer

Examples: Mummelthei, V. "The Sea and the Beloved"

Sentiment Analysis

Definition: Process of determining the emotional tone (positive, negative, or neutral) expressed in text. It can be based on pattern matching (specific words or phrases linked to emotions) or machine learning (models trained on labeled data to recognize sentiment based on context and word relationships).

RQ 1: What emotions are predominant in the Dīwān (poetry collection) of Al-Mutanabbi, and how do they shift across his oeuvre?

RQ 2: What is the emotional tone in 18th-century Islamic critiques of Western colonialism? Are these texts primarily hostile or welcoming toward Western influence?

Tools: Stanford NLP (Arabic, Chinese, Hebrew, etc.), CAMeL Tools (Arabic), LIWC (Arabic, Chinese, Hebrew, etc., payment required)

Disclaimer: Simple tools usually don't support NLS, the above free tools require some programming.

Network Analysis

Definition: A method used to study the relationships and connections between entities (such as people, texts, or ideas) by representing them as a graph.

RQ 1: How are the main characters in Naguib Mahfouz's "The Cairo Trilogy" interconnected, and what does this reveal about the social and political relationships in early 20th-century Egypt?

RQ 2: What role did key figures like Al-Farabi and Avicenna play in the dissemination of Greek philosophy in the Islamic world?

Tools: Gephi

Stylometry

Definition: Statistical analysis of writing style based on machine learning to identify patterns, compare authors, or determine the authorship of anonymous or disputed texts.

RQ 1: Do texts attributed to Ibn Taymiyyah share stylistic patterns with his verified works?

RQ 2: Can we identify stylistic differences between the early and late works of Al-Ghazali?

Tools: R package Stylo (some basic programming needed)

Exploratory Text Analysis

  1. What insights can we get from vocabulary about the works and the author?
    • Domain-specific language usage (e.g., historical, cultural, or political terms).
    • Repeated motifs or symbols that define the worldview or concerns.
    • The author's stylistic tendencies.
  2. How did they change with time?
    • Personal growth or changing views of the author.
    • Response to historical or societal changes.
    • Evolution of genre or audience preferences.

Hypothesis-Driven Text Analysis

Jurji Zaydan

  1. How does Zeydan depict the rise and fall of empires?

    2. Do terms like "الخلافة" (caliphate), "السلطان" (sultan), or "الملك" (king) suggest a critique of leadership or nostalgia for past glory?

  2. What is the relationship between political power and morality?

    3. How often do terms like "العدل" (justice) and "الفساد" (corruption) appear in relation to rulers?

  3. Do words like "الحرب" (war) or "الثورة" (revolution) highlight the inevitability of upheaval in historical progress?

Jane Austen

  1. What social critiques does Austen offer through her portrayal of class and inheritance?

    2. How often do terms like "entailment" and "inheritance" appear in discussions of class and familial conflict?

  2. What expectations does Austen place on male and female characters through her use of language?

    3. Which terms, such as "duty," "honor," or "virtue," are associated with male characters, and how do they shape their roles in the narrative?

    How frequently are terms like "obedience," "delicacy," or "reputation" applied to female characters, and what do they reveal about societal expectations?

  3. How does Austen depict wealth and economic disparity in her works?

    4. What is the frequency of terms like "fortune" and "poverty," and in what contexts do they appear?
